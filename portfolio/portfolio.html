Digital Twin of Ansaldo T100 Micro Gas Turbine

Developed with: Python

This digital twin application simulates the operation of the Ansaldo T100 Micro Gas Turbine. It provides two modes of operation: single-operation mode and dynamic simulation mode.

  • Single-Operation Mode: Allows users to set ambient conditions, desired power, and maximum error. Outputs include operational data for each point and maps for the compressor and turbine.
  • Dynamic Simulation Mode: Real-time operation tracking, including fuel flow rate, efficiency, and power output. Users can change the power setpoint online and observe changes in real-time.

Equations for Single Operation

In single-operation mode, the performance of the micro gas turbine is calculated using the following thermodynamic and aerodynamic equations:

  1. Power Output (P): The net power output of the turbine is calculated using: 
                            P = η_mech × (m_gas × Cp_gas × (T_turbine_in - T_turbine_out))
    Where:
    • η_mech: Mechanical efficiency of the turbine.
    • m_gas: Mass flow rate of the working gas.
    • Cp_gas: Specific heat capacity of the working gas.
    • T_turbine_in: Turbine inlet temperature.
    • T_turbine_out: Turbine outlet temperature.
  2. Compressor Work (W_compressor): The work required to compress the incoming air is calculated as: 
                            W_compressor = m_air × Cp_air × (T_compressor_out - T_compressor_in)
    Where:
    • m_air: Mass flow rate of air.
    • Cp_air: Specific heat capacity of air.
    • T_compressor_out: Compressor outlet temperature.
    • T_compressor_in: Compressor inlet temperature.
  3. Fuel Flow Rate (ṁ_fuel): The fuel flow rate is derived from the energy balance: 
                            ṁ_fuel = P / (η_combustion × LHV)
    Where:
    • η_combustion: Combustion efficiency.
    • LHV: Lower heating value of the fuel.
  4. Thermal Efficiency (η_thermal): The thermal efficiency of the turbine is calculated as: 
                            η_thermal = P / (ṁ_fuel × LHV)
  5. Mass Flow Balance: Ensures mass conservation in the turbine: 
                            m_total = m_air + ṁ_fuel

Key Outputs

The following data is calculated for each operational point:

  • Compressor and turbine maps, including pressure ratio and efficiency curves.
  • Power output and thermal efficiency.
  • Fuel flow rate and combustion parameters.
